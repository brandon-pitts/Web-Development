Art Deco

Painting Industrial Design Architecture

Art Deco as a movement was a popular design style that stretched its influences through the 1920’s and 30’s. The name was derived from the Exposition Internationale des Art Décoratifs et Industriels Modernes which was held in Paris in 1925. Art Deco as an art style can be seen through its architecture, painting, sculpting, and even into industrial design. A key element is that of luxury and Art Deco embodies much of the culture that can be found in the phrase “The roaring twenties” that was used to describe that time period. A key element to Art Deco is an appreciation for the mechanical with a blend of the then modern day high class, this basic ideal can be seen throughout all arms of the movement.

"Among the formative influences on Art Deco were Art Nouveau, the Bauhaus, Cubism, and Serge Diaghilev’s Ballets Russes. Practitioners of Art Deco also found inspiration in American Indian, Egyptian, and early Classical sources as well as from nature."
